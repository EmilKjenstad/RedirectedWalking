REDIRECTED WALKING

Background

Virtual reality enables users to explore worlds in a completely unique way. It places the user into a virtual environment by disconnecting our vision from the real world. However, it comes with some flaws. To move around in the virtual world, the user would often use a joystick or an omni-directional treadmill to move around. Many experiences this as an unnatural way of movements which disconnects us from the virtual world. A solution that feels more natural is therefore desired.

The most natural form of movement would be walking. However, safety issues such as walking into walls or other objects becomes a factor. Additionally, movement in the virtual world would be limited to what we have available in the physical. This is because movements are mapped between the virtual- and physical-world as 1:1; wherever you go in the physical, you go in the virtual.

This is where our research into redirected walking comes in. Redirected walking is a termed used to describe the remapping of position and orientation between the physical- and virtual-world. If done correctly, we can expand the area we can explore in the virtual world. We can do this through manipulating user movements, or by injecting unnoticeable movements to reorient the user.

In this research, we will emphasize on three known methods of reorientation: translation, rotation, and curvature. Our aim is to learn how far we can manipulate the user before the user notices the changes and breaks the immersion.
